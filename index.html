Part A

Timeline

TIMELINE DECEMBER 6, 1881 Butler, Missouri first turns the lights on in its courthouse and for residents – a full nine months before Edison’s Pearl Street Station opens. AUGUST 1909 Electricity generated by the Theodore Roosevelt Dam begins transmission to residents of Phoenix and the Salt River Valley. The project was the first major project of the Bureau of Reclamation 1932 Franklin Roosevelt gives a campaign speech in Portland, Oregon, in which he promotes public power as a right of communities, and a ‘yardstick’ for holding all electric utilities accountable for good service and low rates. NOVEMBER 1965 A blackout affects over 30 million people in all of New York state, New Jersey, across New England, and parts of eastern Canada. The blackout spurs the formation of the North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC) and regional reliability councils 1976 Half of the entities formed this year are Joint Action Agencies 1977 Congress creates the Department of Energy, including the Western Area Power Administration NOVEMBER 1978 Public Utility Regulatory Policies Act (PURPA) is enacted, allowing independent suppliers to compete with utility-owned power generation. 1999 FERC Order 2000 encourages the formation of Regional Transmission Organizations.The Energy Policy Act, the most significant energy law enacted in more than a decade, creates mandatory reliability standards, changes PURPA, repeals PUHCA, and gives FERC tools to prevent market manipulation.

Utilities

What are they and what are they used for?

Future Ideas Utilities are the basic services like your home, apartment, or business needs to keep it comfortable and functioning properly. Common utilities include water, sewer, electric, gas, trash, and recycling. Technology subscriptions like cable TV, internet, security, and phone service can also be considered utilities.
